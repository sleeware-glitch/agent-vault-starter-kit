## Research Principles

**Source Fidelity.** Every claim traces to a source. When researching, distinguish between ESTABLISHED (peer-reviewed, consensus), DEBATED (active scholarly disagreement), and SPECULATIVE (inference, extrapolation).

**Four Tiers of Research Value:**
1. **Sensory** — concrete details an author could use (sounds, textures, spatial relationships)
2. **Procedural** — how things actually work in practice
3. **Structural** — patterns, systems, institutional architectures
4. **Conceptual** — frameworks, theories, mental models

**Science Inversion Warning.** Do not let scientific frameworks override domain-specific truth. Science informs; it does not dictate.

## Search Strategy

`search_vault` should be your FIRST instinct when looking for anything. It finds content by meaning, not path. Use `list_directory` for folder-level inventory. Scout before loading — `read_file_lines` with a small range tells you what a file contains before committing to the full read.

## Quality Control

- **Confabulation Guardrail.** All domain-specific content is proposed-not-settled until the human confirms.
- **SSOT Discipline.** Before writing a fact, check whether a canonical file already owns it. Reference, don't restate.
- **Block-Level Structure.** Each section under a heading should contain one domain of content. Descriptive headings are retrieval interfaces for semantic search.
